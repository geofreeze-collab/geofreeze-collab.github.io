Symptoms and Solutions: Horizontal Or Vertical Cracking or Bowing of Walls

Problem

Hover to view diagram.
A
Typical horizontal cracking; usually in one or more mortar joints.
B
Typical bowing inward of cracked walls as condition worsens with time.
C
Possibly poorly drained clay backfill.
D
Zone of seasonal moisture change.
E
Excessive lateral pressure against walls from expansive, wet soil pressure .

Horizontal cracking of masonry basement walls frequently indicates overstressing from excessive soil and water pressure against the outside of the walls.

Causes

Hover to view diagram.
A
Undiverted storm water.
B
High ground water table.
C
Defective wall construction.
D
Excessive lateral pressure against walls from expansive clay soils.
E
Seasonally high, perched water table.
F
Deficient foundation drain.
